UX Research for a Leading Media Group's Learning Platform

Conducted comprehensive user research and service planning for a major media conglomerate's digital learning ecosystem and membership services, delivering data-driven insights that shaped product and service strategy.

Role Senior UX Researcher
Duration 2022 — 2023
Industry Media & Digital Education
Methods Mixed-Methods Research, Service Planning

Context

A leading media group with decades of editorial authority was expanding its digital learning offerings. As an established content brand, they had built a substantial subscriber base, but their digital learning products and membership ecosystem had grown organically — resulting in fragmented user journeys, unclear value propositions across different service tiers, and untapped opportunities for cross-selling between content verticals.

I was engaged as part of the UX research team to conduct foundational research that would inform both short-term product improvements and long-term service strategy.

Approach

Understanding the Learning Ecosystem

The research scope encompassed two related but distinct areas: the digital learning platform (video courses, professional development content) and the broader membership and subscription system. I conducted mixed-methods research including user interviews with current subscribers across engagement tiers, behavioral data analysis to identify usage patterns and drop-off points, and stakeholder workshops to surface internal assumptions and business constraints.

Service Planning & Opportunity Mapping

Research findings were synthesized into a service planning framework that mapped the current user journey against organizational capabilities and business objectives. This identified specific friction points in the subscription-to-engagement pipeline and highlighted opportunities for the learning platform to strengthen the overall membership value proposition.

Research Scope

  • User research across subscriber segments (new, active, lapsed)
  • Behavioral analytics review for engagement and retention patterns
  • Membership value proposition analysis across service tiers
  • Service blueprint mapping for the learning ecosystem
  • Strategic recommendations for product and membership evolution

Outcomes

The research deliverables provided the product team with a clear, evidence-based picture of how users actually engaged with the learning platform versus how the organization assumed they did. Key recommendations addressed content discovery, onboarding friction, and membership tier clarity — each tied to specific behavioral evidence and prioritized by business impact.

Deliverables

  • User research report with segment-specific insights and behavioral evidence
  • Service planning document mapping opportunities across the membership lifecycle
  • Prioritized recommendations for platform UX improvements and service strategy
  • Frameworks for ongoing research integration into product development
