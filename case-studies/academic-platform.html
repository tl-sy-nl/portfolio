Strategic Roadmap for a B2B Academic Knowledge Platform

As Product Manager, I defined the strategic direction and product roadmap for a B2B academic knowledge platform — identifying differentiation opportunities through design-driven research and cross-functional alignment.

Role Product Manager
Duration Oct 2019 — May 2020
Industry Academic Publishing & EdTech
Focus Product Strategy & Roadmapping

The Challenge

A well-established academic knowledge platform serving universities and research institutions across the Asia-Pacific region needed to re-evaluate its product direction. The competitive landscape was shifting as global platforms expanded into the market, and the existing product had been operating on incremental updates without a cohesive long-term strategy.

I was brought in to define a clear product vision that would differentiate the platform from emerging competitors while serving the evolving needs of academic users — researchers, librarians, and institutional administrators.

My Approach

Research-Driven Product Discovery

I led a design-driven research initiative to understand the unmet needs of key user segments. This involved stakeholder interviews with university librarians and procurement officers, contextual observation of how researchers discover and access academic content, and competitive analysis mapping the feature landscape across regional and global competitors.

Cross-Functional Alignment

A key part of my role was bridging the gap between engineering, business, and user needs. I facilitated cross-functional workshops that translated research findings into shared product priorities, ensuring that the roadmap reflected both market realities and genuine user pain points rather than internal assumptions.

Key Activities

  • User research with researchers, librarians, and institutional administrators
  • Competitive analysis and market positioning assessment
  • Cross-functional prioritization workshops
  • Product roadmap development with differentiation strategy
  • Data-informed strategies for product growth and stakeholder alignment

Outcomes

The resulting roadmap identified specific opportunities for product differentiation — areas where the platform's regional expertise and institutional relationships created defensible advantages against global competitors. Research findings were translated into actionable initiatives that guided product growth, and the strategic framework I established continued to inform product decisions after my departure.

Impact

  • Established a research-informed product strategy and 12-month roadmap
  • Identified key differentiation opportunities based on user needs analysis
  • Aligned engineering, business, and design teams around shared priorities
  • Created a repeatable framework for translating insights into product decisions

Reflection

This was my first product management role, and it shaped how I think about the relationship between research and strategy. I learned that a roadmap isn't a feature list — it's a shared story about where you're going and why. The most valuable thing I brought wasn't design deliverables; it was the ability to sit between disciplines and translate between languages: user language, business language, and technical language.
